# **Evacuation Readiness Review**

**Goal**
Evaluate whether our community road network can support safe evacuation during a wildfire and identify improvements we can pursue as a Firewise community.

---

## Community Characteristics

Our community has:

- A dispersed hillside road network
- Many narrow and winding roads
- Several clusters of homes connected by feeder roads
- Limited exit routes that may create bottlenecks during evacuation

These characteristics are common in wildfire-prone mountain communities and require proactive planning.

---

## Rough Evacuation Estimate

While we have not conducted a formal traffic study, we can estimate evacuation clearance time.

Example planning scenarios:

| Scenario        | Homes      | Vehicles      | Estimated Clearance |
| --------------- | ---------- | ------------- | ------------------- |
| Small event     | ~75 homes  | ~110 vehicles | ~20–30 minutes      |
| Moderate event  | ~100 homes | ~200 vehicles | ~40–60 minutes      |
| Stress scenario | ~125 homes | ~250 vehicles | ~60–75 minutes      |

These estimates assume **mountain road conditions, merging traffic, and wildfire evacuation behavior**.

---

## Likely Evacuation Concerns

Based on the road network shown on the Firewise map:

**Potential bottlenecks**

- Central connector near the “Open Field”
- Northeastern outlet area near parcels 580 / 685 / 800 / 6999
- Interior cluster near parcels 25967 / 25993 / 25961
- Southern road spine serving parcels 26010 / 26115 / 26123

**Other risks**

- Roads with only one exit
- Narrow road segments
- Dense roadside vegetation
- Conflict between evacuation traffic and inbound fire apparatus
- Fallen trees or disabled vehicles blocking routes

---

## Questions for Firewise Leaders

During our discussion we should consider:

- Where would evacuation traffic likely back up first?
- Which roads are narrow or difficult to drive during a fire?
- Which neighborhoods depend on a single evacuation route?
- Which segments could become blocked by falling trees or fire?
- Do residents know their primary and backup evacuation routes?

---

## Possible Improvements

**Near-term actions**

- Roadside vegetation clearance along evacuation routes
- Clear evacuation route maps for residents
- Education about early evacuation
- Identification of road bottlenecks

**Medium-term improvements**

- Turnouts or passing areas on narrow roads
- Improved signage for evacuation routes
- Better turnaround locations for emergency vehicles

**Long-term planning**

- Coordination with Santa Cruz County OES and fire agencies
- Formal evacuation route designation
- Traffic modeling or evacuation study

---

## Proposed Next Steps

1. Identify and map **primary evacuation routes**
2. Identify **top 5 bottlenecks** in the community
3. Estimate **homes and vehicles within the Firewise boundary**
4. Consult with **local fire agencies and county emergency planners**
5. Create a **community evacuation readiness map for residents**
